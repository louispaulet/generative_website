Rabbits

Overview

🐇

Rabbits are small mammals in the family Leporidae of the order Lagomorpha. They are found in several parts of the world and are known for their long ears and hopping gait.

Habitat

🌳

Rabbits inhabit a wide range of environments, from forests and grasslands to deserts and wetlands. They are adaptable creatures and can be found on every continent except Antarctica.

Behavior

🥕

Rabbits are herbivores and primarily feed on grass, leaves, and vegetables. They are known for their digging abilities and create elaborate burrow systems called warrens.

Reproduction

🐰

Rabbits are prolific breeders and have a short gestation period. A female rabbit, called a doe, can give birth to a litter of kittens, usually between 4-12, after a gestation period of about a month.

Conservation

🌍

While some rabbit species are considered pests, others are endangered due to habitat loss and hunting. Conservation efforts are in place to protect vulnerable rabbit populations and their ecosystems.
