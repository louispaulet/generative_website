Turtles

Turtles are reptiles of the order Testudines, characterised by a special bony or cartilaginous shell developed from their ribs and acting as a shield.

Turtles are known for their longevity, with some species living for over a century.

Classification and Evolution

Turtles are divided into two main groups:

  • 🐢 Cryptodira: The hidden-necked turtles
  • 🐢 Pleurodira: The side-necked turtles

Characteristics

Turtles have several unique features:

  • 🐢 Shell: The most distinctive feature of a turtle is its shell, which is made of bone and cartilage.
  • 🐢 Scales: Turtles have scales covering their shells, which provide protection.
  • 🐢 Beak: Turtles have a beak-like mouth, adapted for their herbivorous or carnivorous diet.

Habitat and Distribution

Turtles can be found in various habitats including:

  • 🌴 Forests
  • 🏞️ Freshwater
  • 🌊 Oceans

Conservation

Turtles are threatened by:

  • 🦈 Habitat loss
  • 🐢 Illegal trade
  • 🌊 Pollution
