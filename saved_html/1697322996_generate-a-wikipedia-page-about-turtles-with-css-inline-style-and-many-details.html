Turtles

Turtles are reptiles of the order Testudines. They are characterized by their special bony or cartilaginous shell developed from their ribs, which acts as a shield. Turtles have been around for over 210 million years, making them one of the oldest reptile groups.

Interesting Facts about Turtles:

  • Turtles are known for their longevity. Some species can live up to 150 years or more.
  • They have a unique way of breathing. Turtles have specialized lungs that allow them to extract oxygen from water through their cloaca.
  • Turtles are ectothermic, meaning their body temperature depends on the external environment.
  • There are over 300 different species of turtles across the world, ranging in size from a few centimeters to several meters.
  • Turtles are found in various habitats including oceans, rivers, lakes, and even deserts.

Turtles play important roles in ecosystems. They help control populations of aquatic plants and animals and contribute to nutrient cycling. However, turtles face numerous threats including habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and illegal trade.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these fascinating creatures and ensure their survival for future generations.
